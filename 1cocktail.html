Maui Mule Cocktail

Ingrediants:

  • 1 1/2 ounces KINKY Pink vodka liqueur
  • 1/2 ounce vodka
  • 1/2 ounce fresh lime juice
  • 3 ounces hard ginger beer
  • Crushed ice
  • Lime wedges for garnish
  • Cubed mango for garnish

    Blue Crushed Ice

  • Liquid butterfly pea flower extract
  • Cold water
  • eadible Glitter
  • eadible flowers

How to prepare it:

  1. In a cocktail shaker filled halfway with crushed ice, combine KINKY vodka,
    KINKY liqueur, and lime juice. Shake for 30 seconds.
  2. Fill a clear mule mug with crushed ice. Strain vodka mixture into glass.
    Top with hard ginger beer. Add a layer of crushed blue ice.
    Garnish with fresh mango and lime wedges.
  3. To make the blue ice: add 4-5 drops of liquid butterfly pea flower extract,
    glitter and eadible flowers to each ice cube square in a tray. Fill with water
    & freeze. Crush ice and layer on top of drinks, and watch the magic happen!

    switch out the vodka with tonic water and take a alcohol free version of the ginger beer.

  • Mexican Mule --> with Tequilla

  • Gin Buck --> with gin

  • But in general you can add whatever type of toping to the base (vodka,lime,gingerbeer)

History:

1900s & 1920s:

Sophie Berezinski began spending more time at the workplace with her father in the 1900s and 1920s. The Moscow Copper Co. begins producing copper mugs based on Sophie's design and specifications.
When hard times strike Russia, Sophie resolves to board a ship with 2,000 mugs and sail to America, where she can sell the mugs and live a better life. Sophie relocates to New York City, where she meets her future husband, Max.

1939:

Sophie travels to Hollywood, California to try something new and, maybe, find a market for her stunning copper mugs.

1941:

Sophie creates the first Moscow Mule in her Original Mug at the legendary Cock n' Bull Pub on Sunset Blvd in Hollywood, CA, with two male friends, John and Jack.

1948 - 1950:

A bartender from the Cock n' Bull decides to go out and teach other bartenders how to make the Moscow Mule. He'd walk into a bar with a polaroid camera, order a Moscow Mule, take two photos of the freshly constructed Moscow Mule, and leave one with the bartender. He'd then proceed to the next bar and show them what their competitors were up to, effectively spreading the Moscow Mule throughout the country. We like to think of it as 1940s Social Media.

1951-1966:

The Moscow Mule has become the drink of choice for Hollywood's "it" crowd and "A" listers.

1967:

The nation is hit hard by the Cold War, and the Moscow Mule loses popularity owing to political reasons. Given that the cocktail was conceived in the heart of Hollywood, CA, this is somewhat ironic. With no Moscow Mules to be found, the next 30-40 years were some of America's darkest.

1967-Today:

The Moscow Mule is a popular drink in pubs all around the world. In fact, it recently surpassed the Margarita as America's and arguably the world's most popular cocktail. Sophie Berezinski's legacy goes on at the Moscow Copper Co., where her heirs ensure that everyone has access to the original copper mug that makes the Moscow Mule a true Moscow Mule.
